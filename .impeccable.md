## Design Context

### Users

RotMG (Realm of the Mad God) community members — players, fans, and long-time followers of the game who want to track its health and activity over time. They visit the dashboard out of curiosity or concern about the game's population trends, comparing peaks and valleys across years. They expect data to be trustworthy and legible at a glance, not buried behind complexity. They are comfortable with gaming contexts but do not need hand-holding.

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** Authoritative, Dark, Precise

The interface should feel like a serious data tool built specifically for the RotMG community — not a generic SaaS dashboard and not an over-designed "gamer" site. It earns trust through clarity and restraint. The aggressive red-on-near-black color palette echoes the game's aesthetic without being theatrical. Numbers and charts are the heroes; chrome and decoration serve them.

**Emotional goal:** Confidence in the data. Users should feel they are looking at something built with care and accuracy — not a throwaway stats page.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme:** Dark-first with light mode support. Near-black (`#0a0a0a`) canvas with deep surface layers (`#171717`, `#262626`).
- **Primary accent:** Brand red (`#dc2828`) — used for key actions, data lines, active states, and brand markers. Applied with restraint so it pops when used.
- **Typography:** Russo One for display/headings (bold, game-adjacent personality), Sora for UI and body text (clean, modern), JetBrains Mono for all numeric/tabular data (precision alignment).
- **Motion:** Subtle — card enter animations, skeleton loading pulses, smooth hover transitions (~130ms). No flashy effects.
- **References (feel):** Authoritative data dashboards with personality — precise, structured, trustworthy.
- **Anti-references:**
  - Generic SaaS (Vercel, Linear, Notion aesthetics) — no whites-everywhere, blue CTA buttons, or sans-serif minimalism.
  - Over-the-top "gamer" aesthetics — no RGB gradients, neon, particle effects, or excessive glow.

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero.** Every design decision should serve legibility and trust in the numbers. Decoration earns its place by aiding comprehension, never competing with content.

2. **Restraint with the accent.** Brand red is powerful precisely because it is used sparingly. Reserve it for active states, data series lines, and key brand moments. Dilute it and it loses meaning.

3. **Dark by nature, light by respect.** Dark mode is the primary experience and should feel intentional, not inverted. Light mode is a first-class citizen with the same structure — adapted, not just brightened.

4. **Precision in typography.** Numbers must always be tabular-spaced and monospaced (JetBrains Mono). Display text uses Russo One. Body and UI uses Sora. Never mix these roles.

5. **Subtle motion, never distraction.** Transitions should be fast (≤150ms) and purposeful — confirming state changes, easing layout shifts, or providing loading feedback. No animations that call attention to themselves.
